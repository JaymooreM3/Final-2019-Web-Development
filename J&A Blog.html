  • Scrubs

  • Homemade Skin Care: Honey and Himalayan Salt Body Scrub


    When looking for a new body scrub, there are only a few qualities you need. You want the scrub to work for your skin type, you want it to smell nice, and you want it to be free of toxic, irritating ingredients. That sounds easy enough, but when you go to the store to buy it, you realize quickly that the majority of them have those nasty options and now you are left with only a handful of choices.

  • La Roche-Posay Ultra-Fine Scrub


    If sensitive skin, plus physical exfoliation, sounds like a recipe for disaster, let this gentle scrub by La Roche-Posay change your mind. Super-fine pumice particles — suspended in a cooling gel formula — provide just the right amount of grit without feeling scratchy. Plus, this stuff leaves skin with a quenched suppleness you might expect from a water-based gel moisturizer.

  • Simple Smoothing Facial Scrub


    Oily-skinned folks, listen up. The creamy white exfoliator from Simple contains rice powder to absorb excess oil, and the gentle silica particles — a replacement for microbeads — work to manually slough off dead skin, leaving you positively glowing.

  • Yes To Coconut Energizing Coffee 2-in-1 Scrub & Cleanser Stick


    In case you prefer toting your cleansers around in stick form, consider this portable scrub by Yes to Coconut, which solves any TSA dilemmas and can easily be thrown in a gym bag without worrying about spills. Not to mention, Yes To's coconut and coffee combo smells ah-mazing.
